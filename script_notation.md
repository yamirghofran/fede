1. Scene Headings (Sluglines)
   These indicate a change in location or time.

Notation: Always in ALL CAPS.

Components: Usually starts with INT. (Interior) or EXT. (Exterior), followed by the location, then the time of day.

Example: EXT. RURAL ROAD - DAY.

2. Action Lines
   These describe what is happening on screen and what the characters are doing.

Notation: Written in the present tense, usually left-aligned.

Parser Tip: In this HTML format, action lines are often blocks of text that are not indented as far as dialogue.

Example: "A two-lane road carves through an endless forest of towering black spruce".

3. Character Names
   Identifies who is speaking.

Notation: Written in ALL CAPS and centered (or heavily indented) on the page.

Example: GILMAN or KAYLA.

4. Dialogue
   The words the characters speak.

Notation: Placed directly under the Character Name and centered/indented.

Example: "Where's your survival instincts, boy?".

5. Parentheticals
   Provides context for how a line should be delivered or a small action performed during speech.

Notation: Enclosed in parentheses (...) and usually placed between the Character Name and the Dialogue.

Example: (a ringside announcer) or (SMILING).

6. Transitions
   Instructions for how one scene moves to the next.

Notation: Usually in ALL CAPS and right-aligned.

Example: CUT TO: or CONTINUED:.

7. Extensions
   Extra information next to a character's name about the audio.

O.S. (Off-Screen): The character is physically there but not visible.

V.O. (Voice-Over): The character is narrating or speaking from a different time/place.

Example: STRYKER (O.S.).
